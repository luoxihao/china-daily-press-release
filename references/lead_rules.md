# Lead Rules

## Preferred Lead Shapes

1. Event scale first: who participated, how many, where, and when.
2. Outcome first: what changed, opened, launched, concluded, or was announced.
3. Cultural significance first: use only when the cultural point is concrete and verified.
4. International participation first: useful for exchange, sports, tourism, and conference stories.
5. City/tourism development first: use when the program has verified service, route, consumption, or policy details.

## Required Lead Tests

- The lead answers at least three core questions: who, what, when, where, why, or how.
- The lead contains the strongest verified news point.
- The lead avoids unexplained local shorthand.
- The lead does not merely restate the official event name.
- The lead is one or two sentences.

## Rewrite Pattern

Weak: `The 2026 X activity was successfully held in Y.`

Better: `More than [number] participants from [places] joined [event] in [location] on [date], combining [verified element] with [verified element].`
