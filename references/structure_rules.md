# Structure Rules

## Default English Article Structure

1. Headline
2. Optional subheadline
3. Lead with the central news fact
4. Event details: scale, date, place, organizers, participants
5. Route, program, product, or activity details
6. Quote or paraphrased source-backed explanation
7. Cultural, historical, sports, tourism, or industry background
8. International-reader explanation
9. Broader significance or future plan when sourced
10. Fact-check and pending-confirmation notes

## Paragraph Rules

- Keep paragraphs short and scannable.
- One paragraph should do one job.
- Put technical or administrative details after the reader knows why the story matters.
- Use transitions sparingly; let facts create the flow.

## Common Module Types

- Fact module: verified event facts.
- Human module: participant, visitor, expert, or organizer perspective.
- Context module: history, policy, industry, or cultural background.
- International module: why the event matters to non-local readers.
- Risk module: pending facts, source gaps, or naming issues.
