# Fact Check Protocol

## Information That Must Be Verified

- Event name in Chinese and English
- Date, time, and location
- Organizer, co-organizer, host, sponsor, and implementing bodies
- Participant numbers, countries, regions, guests, speakers, winners, and rankings
- Quotes and paraphrased claims
- Historical background, heritage status, and policy references
- Financial data, tourism revenue, visitor numbers, investment, and economic impact
- Awards, certifications, rankings, and "first" claims
- Route, schedule, project list, and future plans
- Photo captions and credits

## Information That Must Not Be Fabricated

- Direct quotes
- Speaker identities or titles
- Institutions or organizer relationships
- Dates, times, schedules, routes, race results, and awards
- Data, percentages, rankings, visitor numbers, countries, and revenue
- UNESCO status, policy status, official recognition, or publication status
- Emotional reactions from participants unless provided

## Missing Information Handling

- Use `[to be confirmed]` for missing but necessary facts.
- Use `[source needed]` for claims that require external proof.
- Use `[official English name to be confirmed]` for uncertain names.
- Use `[approved quote needed]` when a quote would improve the story but no quote is supplied.
- If missing facts are central to the story, produce a fact-gap report before drafting.

## Conflicting Information Handling

- List each conflicting claim with its source.
- Do not choose the more convenient fact.
- Use the narrower, better-sourced claim only if clearly supported.
- Otherwise mark the item as conflicting and exclude it from the article body.

## Direct Quote Handling

- Use a direct quote only when supplied by the user or a clearly cited source.
- Do not turn a paraphrase into quotation marks.
- Do not polish a quote so heavily that it changes meaning.
- If translating a quote, preserve meaning and mark the source language when needed.

## Absolute Claims

Flag or remove unsupported:

- first
- largest
- best
- world-class
- unprecedented
- unique
- landmark achievement
- iconic unless explained as a known landmark

## Numbers And Dates

- Every number that affects meaning needs a source.
- Convert units for international readers when useful.
- Keep exact event dates; use broader dates only for nonessential background.
- Avoid mixing registered participants, starters, finishers, visitors, and audience without distinction.

## Place And Institution Names

- Confirm official English names from source materials or authoritative websites.
- Explain district, county, province, and scenic-area geography when needed.
- Avoid local shorthand that international readers cannot place.

## Pending Confirmation Output Format

```markdown
## Pending Confirmation

| Item | Why it matters | Current status | Suggested source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Official English event name | Needed for headline and first reference | [to be confirmed] | Organizer release |
```
