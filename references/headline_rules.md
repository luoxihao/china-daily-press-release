# Headline Rules

## Use

- Place plus action: `Tianjin expands sports tourism appeal`
- Event plus outcome: `Marathon draws international runners to Great Wall route`
- Cultural object plus public experience: `Heritage fair brings traditional crafts to new audiences`
- City plus development angle: `Wuxi conference showcases cultural tourism sector`
- Human-interest feature wording when the story is softer and source-backed.

## Avoid

- `Successfully held`
- `Grandly opens`
- `Injects strong momentum`
- `Shows infinite charm`
- `Creates a world-class miracle`
- Any claim that cannot be sourced in the article.

## Checks

- Does the headline name a concrete action, event, place, or outcome?
- Can the headline be understood by an international reader without local shorthand?
- Would the headline still be accurate if stripped of all promotional adjectives?
