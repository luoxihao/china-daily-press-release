# Copyright And Attribution Policy

## Research Use Only

Public China Daily articles in this skill are used only to study transferable style tendencies: headline patterns, lead structure, attribution habits, paragraph flow, background placement, and tone.

## What Is Stored

- URL
- Article title
- Publication date when available
- Section or column when available
- Topic category
- Article type
- Structure summary
- Style observations
- Do-not-copy note

## What Is Not Stored

- Full article text
- Long excerpts
- Distinctive paragraphs
- Reusable article-specific wording
- Unlicensed photos, captions, or media assets

## Drafting Rules

- Final output must be newly written from user-provided facts and verified source materials.
- Do not create a paragraph highly similar to a public article.
- Do not copy article sequencing if it is distinctive to one story.
- Do not imply China Daily wrote, approved, authorized, reviewed, endorsed, or will publish the draft.

## Attribution Rules

- Attribute public facts to their sources when used.
- Keep direct quotes only when the quote is supplied by the user or clearly sourced and appropriate for reuse.
- If a public article inspired a style observation, cite the article URL in research notes, not in the drafted article unless the article is a factual source.
