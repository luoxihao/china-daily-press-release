# China Daily Public Corpus Style Analysis

Research date: 2026-06-13

Corpus notes are stored in `china_daily_corpus_notes.md`. The corpus uses public China Daily article metadata and style observations only; it does not store full article text.

## Common Headline Patterns

- Place plus action: `Tianjin sets sights...`, `Xinjiang aims...`, `Wuxi conference showcases...`
- Event plus outcome: marathon opens, festival celebrates, expo opens, event kicks off.
- Human-interest or feature-style wording for softer stories.
- Culture-tourism headlines often name the location and the cultural object or event.
- Sports headlines often foreground runners, routes, participation, or the event name.
- Headlines generally avoid bare slogans and heavy official language.

## Common Lead Patterns

- Start with the event outcome or concrete activity.
- For tourism and city stories, connect the event to visitor experience, cultural resources, consumption, or city development.
- For sports stories, foreground scale, place, participants, date, route, and competition format.
- For heritage stories, start with preservation, visitor access, digital presentation, or cultural interpretation.
- The lead is usually concise and followed quickly by supporting context.

## Common Body Structure

- Paragraph 1: news fact or event outcome.
- Paragraphs 2-3: scale, organizer, location, participants, or program details.
- Middle paragraphs: quotes, background, cultural explanation, policy context, or industry context.
- Later paragraphs: broader significance, tourism/culture/sports integration, public participation, international exchange, or future plans.
- Paragraphs remain short and scannable.

## Background Placement

- Background rarely dominates the lead.
- Historical or cultural background usually appears after the news event is established.
- Heritage articles explain why a place or object matters, often with location, history, preservation status, or public-access details.
- City-promotion articles place development goals after concrete programs or activities.

## Quote Usage

- Quotes are generally used after the factual setup.
- Officials or organizers provide policy, development, or event-purpose framing.
- Participants, visitors, experts, or artists provide experience, interpretation, or human texture.
- Direct quotes are not needed for every short release; paraphrase is common when facts are stronger than sentiment.

## Cultural Explanation Strategy

- Explain Chinese place names, customs, heritage forms, and institutions with reader-friendly context.
- Link local culture to visitor experience, public participation, youth interest, international exchange, or preservation.
- Avoid assuming overseas readers know administrative geography or historical references.
- Treat culture as observable practice, not abstract slogan.

## Sports, Tourism, And City-Promotion Expression

- Sports stories connect route, participation, scenery, organization, and destination appeal.
- Tourism stories connect activities to visitor flows, consumption, local industries, and service upgrades.
- City-promotion stories use events as evidence of opening-up, cultural vitality, or international engagement.
- The tone is positive but supported by facts, names, and concrete actions.

## International Reader Adaptation

- Provide the country, province, city, district, or landmark context when needed.
- Explain why a local event matters beyond local administrative routine.
- Use internationally understandable categories: marathon, heritage site, cultural festival, expo, trade fair, tourism route, public fitness.
- Convert or explain Chinese-specific units, names, and policy language.

## Common Endings

- Broader development context.
- A future program or policy plan when sourced.
- A heritage, tourism, sports, or people-to-people exchange significance.
- A concluding fact rather than a slogan.

## Risks Not To Imitate

- Do not reproduce any article's wording or paragraph sequence.
- Do not overuse "boost", "vitality", "showcase", or "highlight" without concrete facts.
- Do not turn a news item into tourism advertising.
- Do not use a public article's quote unless it is properly sourced and permitted for the target use.
- Do not state that a draft is official China Daily content.
